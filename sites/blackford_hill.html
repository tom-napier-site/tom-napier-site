Basic information

Blackford hill TX as seen from the South-East. src Thomas Probyn

Blackford Hill is a former BT microwave site, which is now used as a modern microwave link site, as well as a host tower for BR (business radio) license holders to install their equipment. Local shops, universities and other enterprises install radio base stations here to act as repeaters for handheld radio equipment of the type used by security guards.

Quick facts

Year built: 1951
Purpose BT Microwave television link
Tower style Originally BT pylon, now police radio style

History

Blackford has something of a pedegree as a microwave site. Originally a part of the BT Microwave network, a link on the Manchester to Kirk-O-Shotts television route, which took television from the Granada area to the Lothians via the East coast of Northern England. When it was replaced by the nearby (and now nonexistant) Craiglockhart site, the BT microwave dishes were removed, but a photograph of the site remains on the BT archive, as well as the dgsys website, pictured below.

Image: Blackford as-was in 1951 (src dgsys, original from BT Archive)

Compared to its counterparts in other parts of the country, Blackford is a relatively minimal installation. The mast does not appear to have served any alterior purposes other than simply linking TV signals (encoded as microwaves) across the landscapes. That is to say, unlike some other sites, this tower never created a signal which could be picked up by viewers, or a link out to a site which could, it was simply a link in a chain. Eventually, the signal would reach Black Hill transmitter, near Airdrie, which would broadcast the resultant signal to the masses.

At the foot of the mast sits a rather standard 1950s era transmitter hall which held the equipment which would perform demodulation, amplification and remodulation of the incoming microwave signal. A photograph survives in the BT archive of the insides of these buildings.
